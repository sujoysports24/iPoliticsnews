Advanced sensors and features<\/p>\n

Technological advances are driving the global market for smart wearables, electronic devices worn on the body that connect to the Internet and smartphones. With the growth of smartphones and the Internet of Things (IoT), wearables are becoming an increasingly popular way to collect data, monitor health, and connect with the environment. One of the key technological improvements driving the wearable business is the development of smaller and more powerful sensors and processors. These improvements have made it possible to build wearables that can measure a wide range of biometric data, including heart rate, blood pressure, and even brain waves. In addition, the development of new materials and manufacturing processes is also a key driver of the wearable market on a global level. These advances have enabled the development of wearables that are more comfortable, durable and fashionable than ever before.<\/p>\n

Many companies target different demographics, such as child-centric, gender-specific, and disabled. The company\u2019s product, the Dot Watch, is a Braille smartwatch that uses Braille tactile cells to display notifications, messages, and time, making information self-accessible to people with visual impairments. The company received his US$500,000 grant from the South Korean government\u2019s Tech Incubator Program for Startups (TIPS) and his US$100,000 grant from the ActnerLab accelerator. Adoption of smartwatches and fitness bands increases<\/p>\n

Growing consumer interest in fitness and wellness is one of the main drivers of the smart wearables industry. People are becoming more health conscious and want to know how to measure their physical activity to improve their overall health. Smart wearables are becoming increasingly popular because they can track exercise, monitor health, and quickly access information while on the move. These devices are not only stylish, they are also efficient with features like heart rate monitoring, sleep tracking and calorie tracking. Furthermore, the introduction of the latest technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning is accelerating the evolution of smart grids.<\/p>\n

Smart apparel and clothing gaining popularity<\/p>\n

Growing applications of smart clothing in various industries such as sports and fitness, healthcare, military, and defense are driving the global smart clothing market. Over the projected period, the growing need to monitor physical activity via sensors is likely to drive demand for smart clothing. According to a recent World Economic Forum (WEF) poll, 92.1% of business owners expect 10% of people to wear internet-connected clothing by 2025. In addition, newly created advanced fibers such as nanofibers and hybrid materials may be used. To promote the smart clothing industry in the coming years.<\/p>\n

Impact of the novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19)<\/p>\n

The global smart wearables market has been greatly impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2020, global market sales declined due to supply chain disruptions and retail store closures. However, the pandemic has underscored the importance of health monitoring, driving demand for smartwatches and fitness trackers that can measure vital metrics such as heart rate, oxygen saturation and body temperature. Remote patient monitoring is also growing in popularity, with healthcare workers deploying smart wearables to remotely monitor patients and reduce the chance of viral transmission.<\/p>\n

Effects of the Russo-Ukrainian War<\/p>\n

The Russo-Ukrainian war has disrupted supply chains at the global level. Many smart wearable devices are made in China, a major supplier of electronic components. But China is a close ally of Russia, and the war cut off supply lines for these parts. The result has been component shortages, smart wearable manufacturing delays, and price hikes. In addition, the war forced many manufacturers to relocate. This could be an opportunity for other countries to strengthen their manufacturing capacity and become more competitive in the global smart wearables market.<\/p>\n
