Smart wearables have exploded in popularity around the world. From health trackers to smartwatches, these products help individuals stay connected in unimaginable ways. The global smart wearables market size is valued at USD 72.19 billion in 2022 and is expected to reach USD 231.82 billion in 2030, at a CAGR of 15.7% over the forecast period from 2023 to 2030. grow in The market is expected to maintain its current growth rate. Increased adoption of IoT and connected devices, increased awareness of health and fitness, and expansion of e-commerce platforms will lead to further growth in the years that follow. Moreover, increasing usage of smart wearables in the healthcare industry is boosting the market. Healthcare facilities are working to use wearable devices to check a patient’s vital signs and give advance warning if a problem arises. In addition, some wearables are intended to help people with chronic diseases cope with difficulties. These devices can track medication adherence and symptom intensity and provide personalized recommendations and assistance.

With the growing demand for wearable devices in the enterprise environment and increased investment in wearable technology start-ups, there is plenty of room for expansion. In addition, increasing use of wearable devices in virtual and augmented reality applications and increasing demand for fashion and luxury wearables have boosted the demand for smart wearables market on a global level. Additionally, the shift to remote work and online education has increased the demand for smart wearables that can not only detect physical activity but also offer features such as sleep tracking and stress monitoring.

Advanced sensors and features

Technological advances are driving the global market for smart wearables, electronic devices worn on the body that connect to the Internet and smartphones. With the growth of smartphones and the Internet of Things (IoT), wearables are becoming an increasingly popular way to collect data, monitor health, and connect with the environment. One of the key technological improvements driving the wearable business is the development of smaller and more powerful sensors and processors. These improvements have made it possible to build wearables that can measure a wide range of biometric data, including heart rate, blood pressure, and even brain waves. In addition, the development of new materials and manufacturing processes is also a key driver of the wearable market on a global level. These advances have enabled the development of wearables that are more comfortable, durable and fashionable than ever before.

Target dynamic demographics such as people with disabilities

Many companies target different demographics, such as child-centric, gender-specific, and disabled. For example, South Korean startup Dot Inc. is a company focused on developing smart wearables for visually impaired individuals. The company’s product, the Dot Watch, is a Braille smartwatch that uses Braille tactile cells to display notifications, messages, and time, making information self-accessible to people with visual impairments. The company received his US$500,000 grant from the South Korean government’s Tech Incubator Program for Startups (TIPS) and his US$100,000 grant from the ActnerLab accelerator. Therefore, market players in this industry are targeting different demographics to gain market share, thus enabling market expansion.

government efforts

Governments in many countries are promoting the use of smart wearables. As Japan’s healthcare spending rises, the government is adopting various methods to save costs by increasing R&D investment and introducing regulations on digital health products and services. Recent trends indicate that Japanese customers are more interested in cutting-edge products from US digital health companies. The smartphone usage rate in Japan is 90.8% among those aged 13-19 and 99.0% among those aged 20-29. The majority of Japanese teens and young adults, including university students aged 18-22, use smartphones all day long. For this reason, the Japanese government has focused on implementing various strategies to boost demand for smart wearable products in the country.

Adoption of smartwatches and fitness bands increases

Growing consumer interest in fitness and wellness is one of the main drivers of the smart wearables industry. People are becoming more health conscious and want to know how to measure their physical activity to improve their overall health. Smart wearables are becoming increasingly popular because they can track exercise, monitor health, and quickly access information while on the move. These devices are not only stylish, they are also efficient with features like heart rate monitoring, sleep tracking and calorie tracking. Furthermore, the introduction of the latest technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning is accelerating the evolution of smart grids.

Smart apparel and clothing gaining popularity

Growing applications of smart clothing in various industries such as sports and fitness, healthcare, military, and defense are driving the global smart clothing market. Over the projected period, the growing need to monitor physical activity via sensors is likely to drive demand for smart clothing. According to a recent World Economic Forum (WEF) poll, 92.1% of business owners expect 10% of people to wear internet-connected clothing by 2025. In addition, newly created advanced fibers such as nanofibers and hybrid materials may be used. To promote the smart clothing industry in the coming years.

Impact of the novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19)

The global smart wearables market has been greatly impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2020, global market sales declined due to supply chain disruptions and retail store closures. However, the pandemic has underscored the importance of health monitoring, driving demand for smartwatches and fitness trackers that can measure vital metrics such as heart rate, oxygen saturation and body temperature. Remote patient monitoring is also growing in popularity, with healthcare workers deploying smart wearables to remotely monitor patients and reduce the chance of viral transmission.

Effects of the Russo-Ukrainian War

The Russo-Ukrainian war has disrupted supply chains at the global level. Many smart wearable devices are made in China, a major supplier of electronic components. But China is a close ally of Russia, and the war cut off supply lines for these parts. The result has been component shortages, smart wearable manufacturing delays, and price hikes. In addition, the war forced many manufacturers to relocate. This could be an opportunity for other countries to strengthen their manufacturing capacity and become more competitive in the global smart wearables market.

Status and prospects of major companies

The global smart wearables market is highly competitive, and market players are looking for a competitive edge in this rapidly growing industry, primarily by catering to the millennial demographic.

Leading companies in the industry employ various advertising strategies to effectively reach target audiences, increase brand awareness and drive product sales. Collaboration and influencer marketing are widely adopted by companies today. For example, Fitbit has partnered with fitness influencers and big-name artists such as Will His Smith and Jules Sebastian to promote their Fitness His trackers and smartwatches. Additionally, the company collaborates with TV shows, movies, and popular events to showcase its products. One example is his use of Fitbit devices in his TV series like “Brooklyn Nine-Nine.” It shows the character using his Fitbit products as part of his daily life.
